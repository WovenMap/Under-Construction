§01 · the nameWovenMap.

Capital W. Capital M. The wordmark renders lowercase — wovenmap — but in body copy and any written reference, the brand is written WovenMap. One token, two halves, joined.

Spelling rules. "WovenMap" in writing (camel-case, two capitals). "wovenmap" in the wordmark (lowercase, all-one). Never: "Wovenmap", "Woven Map", "Woven-Map", "WOVENMAP" (only acceptable shouted in tagline ALL-CAPS lockup). The product is one word, not two.

§02 · the locked sentenceWhat we say first.

The brand has one positioning sentence — kept verbatim across every public surface — and one tagline, kept as a refrain.

The locked sentence

Everyone's running on a hidden web of people, projects and promises. WovenMap shows you yours.

The tagline

See what's really going on.

The tagline is plain-spoken on purpose. It is not a metaphor. It is what the user is actually asking when they pick up the app: what is really going on. The brand answers that question with the quiet honesty of a good map.

Acceptable variants

  • "The map you didn't know you were walking on." — long-form headline.
  • "WovenMap. See your weave." — short headline lockup.
  • "A private map of an actual life." — explainer line.

§05 · the markWoven crosshatch · eight bearings.

Four threads cross through a centre and end in eight bearings — the cardinal and intercardinal points of a compass. Where the early concept showed a flat asterisk, the production mark adds weave: each thread curves slightly, suggesting fabric on a frame. The centre is a single dot, the user's you-are-here.

Production · 200 × 200
Favicon · simplified · 32–64px

Construction rules

  • Four threads, each a single Bézier curve from one bearing to the opposite.
  • Inner crosshatch — four short straight strokes — gives the centre its woven feel; drops on favicon size.
  • Eight endpoint dots: cardinal (N, E, S, W) at r=4; intercardinal (NE, SE, SW, NW) at r=3. The hierarchy reads as a compass.
  • Centre dot at r=4 with optional faint r=10 ring — the you-are-here.
  • Stroke weights: 2.0 on the four threads, 1.5 on the crosshatch. Halve at small sizes.
  • Optional outer compass ticks at the four cardinals — drop below 100px.
  • Single-colour mark only. Never bicolour. The colour is champagne on dark, ink on light.

§07 · cartographic vocabularyWhat the new metaphor adds.

The shift from weave to WovenMap brings a second layer of imagery — cartography — alongside the textile language we already own. The two layers compound; they don't compete.

Words available to the brand

a bearing a sounding a contour a legend a scale a chart a compass rose a meridian an isobar a marker a route a station

Where they show up

  • Lens deep-views get a faint contour-line backdrop (topographic, not decorative).
  • Q&A chat draws on the sounding metaphor — "let's take a sounding on this."
  • Digest narrative uses cartographic prose: "This week your weave shifted bearing on three threads."
  • The web view may show a faint compass-rose at far zoom; bearings (N, E, S, W) become navigation affordances.
  • Onboarding uses route language — first leg, waypoint, landfall.

Where they don't

  • Body copy in technical specs (modules-deep, ai-interactions, data-model). The textile and the technical do the work; cartography is a flavour, not a swap.
  • Error states. ("You've gone off course" reads as scolding; keep "Something didn't work — try again.")
  • Marketing copy that risks sounding nautical-cosplay.

§08 · voice & tonePlain-spoken. Slightly literary. Never glib.

The brand voice does not change with the rebrand. The instinct that ran through every existing document still runs through this one: say the true thing, calmly, in the fewest words it deserves.

We do

  • Use specific nouns. "Your weave" not "your data."
  • Use plain verbs. "Shows." Not "empowers."
  • Stay quiet. Whitespace is part of the voice.
  • Trust the reader. Don't over-explain a metaphor that's working.
  • Allow a touch of literary cadence in headlines; never in error states.

We don't

  • Use words like "empower", "unleash", "supercharge", "AI-powered."
  • Use exclamation marks. (Anywhere. Ever.)
  • Personify the AI. The AI is a tool, not a friend.
  • Promise certainty. Promise better-than-nothing; deliver most-of-the-time.
  • Lean on the cartography metaphor when textile language is already saying it.

§09 · applicationHow it lands.

Hero / brochure surfaces

Night palette. Champagne mark. Locked sentence in Fraunces 300, italic for the WovenMap fragment. Tagline below in Inter caps, ledger-spaced. White space is the design.

Long-form spec / editorial documents

Day palette. Ink mark. Newsreader body, Fraunces headlines, IBM Plex Mono metadata. Existing companion docs (requirements.html, modules-deep.html, ai-interactions.html, data-model.html, inter-weave.html, build-plan.html, naming-deep-dive.html) all stay in this register. Only the brand name changes.

In-app surfaces

Day palette by default; user-toggle to night. Champagne becomes the in-app accent in night mode (not amber). The four lens accents are unchanged in either mode.

Marketing collateral

Mostly night palette. Photography (when used) is shot in low warm light to harmonise with champagne. No stock imagery. Illustration only — line work, threads, contour, compass.
